The impact of (UI) & (UX) on user decisions

User experience is a critical factor in making decisions within a website or app. A good design promotes engagement and loyalty, while a bad experience drives users to leave quickly.

Article

How does design control user decisions?

Ⅰ
First impressions are quick but crucial!

A haphazard design can lose a user within seconds.

  •    Use color consistency, clear fonts, and a clean structure to attract the user.
Ⅱ
Ease of use means faster decisions

Complex design confuses the user and makes them hesitate.

  •   Make paths clear with prominent buttons and easy-to-navigate menus.
Ⅲ
Color and psychology

Colors influence emotions and push the user to react.

  • Like:
       Blue for confidence and stability.
  •    Red for enthusiasm and urgency.
Ⅳ
Fitts' Law and interaction

This law determines how users interact with buttons and interactive elements

  •   Make important buttons larger and more accessible, especially in mobile apps.
Ⅴ
Emotional Design

Emotional design is based on evoking certain emotions that drive the user to relate to the website or application:

  •    Use images and illustrations that express positive and friendly feelings.
  •    Add interactive touches like microanimation to make the experience more enjoyable.
Ⅵ
The importance of images and visuals

The user decides within a few seconds whether to stay or leave the page based on visual elements.

  •   Use high-quality images and engaging illustrations to increase emotional connection.
Ⅶ
Microinteractions

Those little movements that occur when you click a button, scroll your mouse, or receive a notification.

  • Why is it important?
      Make interaction more visible and transparent.
  •   User guidance and improved navigation.

Conclusion

Don't assume that your design is always the best! Use A/B tests to see what works best, a simple test can help you improve the user experience and increase your conversion rate by 30%. A seamless and engaging experience means greater engagement and stronger loyalty, driving users to take the desired action instead of leaving the site.
